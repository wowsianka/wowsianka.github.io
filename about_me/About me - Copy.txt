PROJECT 2: AI Research - Deep Learning for CCR Exposure Prediction ?
Brief Version (Interview):
"This is an AI research initiative to accelerate our CCR calculations by replacing slow Monte Carlo simulations with fast neural networks. Monte Carlo is accurate but creates a bottleneck for daily reporting and real-time analytics – it's too slow and resource-heavy.
I'm developing bidirectional LSTM networks that learn the complex non-linear mapping between market risk factors and exposure metrics like Expected Exposure, PFE, and default probabilities. The model takes inputs like interest rates, volatilities, credit spreads, simulated MtM paths, shocked market conditions, and counterparty characteristics – and predicts EE/EPE/PFE directly.
The key use cases are: quantile exposure forecasting under stress, path-wise exposure prediction with shocked scenarios, trade-level forecasting that aggregates to counterparty level, and default probability prediction.

Target Outputs:

PROJECT 4: Stressed EAD Forecasting for Equities ?
Brief Version (Interview):
"This is a predictive model for daily stressed Exposure at Default forecasting for equities under regulatory capital frameworks – specifically Basel III and FRTB. It supports capital optimization, proactive stress scenario planning, and accelerates our capital planning cycles.
I'm using linear regression on return-based features – not levels – to handle non-stationarity better. The features include: market risk factor returns across FX, equity indices, and interest rate curves; weekly exposure aggregates; time-based patterns like month-end and quarter-end effects; and autoregressive lags at 1, 2, 3, 5, and 10 days to capture momentum and mean reversion.
The model predicts log returns, then reconstructs levels using bias correction and calibration to align with actual exposure trajectories. It's currently in production supporting daily risk reporting."
DETAILED - If They Ask Follow-Ups:
Business Context:
? Purpose: Regulatory capital optimization (Basel III/FRTB)
? Outputs: Daily stressed EAD forecasts under adverse scenarios
? Benefits: Capital efficiency, proactive hedging, automated stress testing
Modeling Approach:
? Target: Log returns (not levels) ? handles non-stationarity, heteroskedasticity
? Model: Linear regression (interpretable baseline)
? Post-processing: Bias correction + calibration to reconstruct levels
? Validation: Alignment with actual exposure trajectories
Why Linear Regression? "I started with linear regression because it's interpretable and serves as a strong baseline. Once I validate the feature engineering pipeline—lags, cyclical encodings, return transformations—I can benchmark more complex models. For time-series forecasting with non-linear patterns, I'd consider XGBoost or LSTM networks, but for this use case, linear regression with well-engineered features delivers comparable accuracy with better transparency for regulators."
